The Prohibition of Love



The Prohibition of Love is an opera in two acts, written and composed by Richard Wagner in 1834, the second by the composer and the first to be performed. The libretto is based on the comedy Measure for Measure, by William Shakespeare. She was described by Wagner himself as a "grosse komische Oper"
