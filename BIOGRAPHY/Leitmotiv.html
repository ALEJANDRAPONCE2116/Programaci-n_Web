Leitmotiv

is a "short, constantly recurring musical phrase" associated with a particular person, place, or idea. It is closely related to the musical concepts of idée fixe or motto-theme. The spelling leitmotif is an anglicization of the German Leitmotiv, literally meaning "leading motif", or "guiding motif". A musical motif has been defined as a "short musical idea ... melodic, harmonic, or rhythmic, or all three", a salient recurring figure, musical fragment or succession of notes that has some special importance in or is characteristic of a composition: "the smallest structural unit possessing thematic identity."

Example of leitmotiv: Renuntiation of love



When the nymphs of the water warn Alberich:



"Nur wer der Minne Macht entsagt,
nur wer der Liebe Lust verjagt,
nur der erzielt sich den Zauber,
zum Reif zu zwingen das Gold".

"Only those who renounce the power of love,
only those who reject the pleasure of love,
only he achieves the prodigy
to force gold to become a ring".




When Alberich tells how he had to give up love to forge the ring :





"Wie ich der Liebe abgesagt,
alles, was lebt,
soll ihr entsagen!".


"How I canceled love,
everything that lives
should you renounce!".





When Sigmund makes an affirmation of love:



"Heiligster Minne höchste Not,
sehnender Liebe < sehrende Not)".

"Extreme need of sacred love,
aching need of longing love".




When Wotan renuncied gives up Brünnhilde:



"Denn so kehrt der Gott sich dir ab,…".

"Well that's how it departs from you the god, ...".




When Brünnhilde don't want to gives up the ring than Siegfried gave her, because represent the love of the couple:



"die Liebe liesse ich nie,
mir nähmen nie sie die Liebe".

"I will never turn away from love
they would never take away my love,".
